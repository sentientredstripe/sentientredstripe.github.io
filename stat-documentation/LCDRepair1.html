  1. HP G4/G5 Chromebook

LCD Repair

The HP Chromebooks are great until one comes in with a broken LCD. Then, you get to spend 30 minutes questioning every single design decision that HP made when they designed their Chromebooks.


Before repairing any device, verify that the student whose Chromebook you are about to repair does not have insurance.


Materials:

  • Screwdriver
  • Pry tool
  • New HP LCD
  • Parts tray


Time: 20-30 minutes


  1. Turn the device off if you have not already done so.
  2. Turn the device over and locate the 11 small rubber covers that sit flush with the bottom of the device. There are 10 on the perimeter and one in the center-top of the device near the label.
  3. Using something sharp and pointy, stab the rubber covers and pull them off. They are glued on but should come off easily. Save them.
  4. Remove the 11 Phillips-head screws from the bottom of the computer.
  5. Flip the device over.
  6. Flip the device back over. You forgot to remove a screw. There are 11 screws. Count them. You forgot the one in the center-top. It's slightly off center.
  7. Flip the device back over and open it.
  8. Take the flat end of your pry tool or a small flathead screwdriver and insert it at the seam between the keyboard deck and the body of the computer, then pry up.
    1. The keyboard is held on by friction and plastic clips. Repeat this process going around the device.
  9. Lift the keyboard up slightly so it comes out of the body and then open it as if it was the lid of the laptop. You will need to disconnect the keyboard and touchpad ribbon cables.
  10. Lift the latches that hold the keyboard and touchpad ribbon cables in place and remove them. Place the keyboard off to the side.
  11. Locate and disconnect the display cable. Do not disconnect the WiFi antennas (as they are a royal pain to reconnect).
  12. Locate and unscrew the hinges from the base of the device. Be sure not to let it fall back, as this could accidentally disconnect the WiFi antennas.
  13. The WiFi antenna cables have enough slack in them that you can remove the display assembly from the body and lay it flat on the work surface without disconnecting them.
  14. On the display assembly, locate the two black stickers at the bottom of the display assembly.
    2. They are to either side of the bulge of the hinge and at the extreme left and extreme right of the bottom of the display assembly.
  15. Slide something small and pointy between the sticker and frame of the assembly, and peel it off. Save these.
  16. Under each sticker is a single screw. Remove it.
  17. Now, take the flat end of your pry tool or a flathead screwdriver, insert it between the bezel and the base of the display, and pry up. Repeat this process to remove the bezel. Feel free to use your hands once there is enough space for them, it will make the process quicker.
  18. Locate the four Phillips-head screws holding down the display and remove them.
  19. The display will fold over onto the keyboard to reveal the back of the display and the display connector.
  20. Remove the piece of tape holding the display connector in place and disconnect the display.
  21. Place your new display in the same position the old display was in when you disconnected the cable.  Plug in the new display and fold it up into the display assembly, making sure that the display cable is tucked into its channel.
  22. Reattach the four screws that hold the LCD in place and remove the protective covering from the LCD.
  23. Attach the bezel, making sure to snap all of the clips back into place.  It may take some force, but don't worry about hurting anything.
  24. Replace the two screws that go in the bottom of the bezel and the stickers that go over them.
  25. Taking the display assembly, line the hinges up to their mounts on the base and replace the screws that hold them in.
  26. Reconnect the display and power on the device. This can be accomplished by plugging in the device or by temporarily reconnecting the keyboard to the motherboard. If everything works correctly, make sure both the WiFi antenna and display cables are properly routed in their channels.
  27. Reconnect the keyboard and touchpad ribbons, and reattach the keyboard to the base. As it is held on by clips and friction, simply apply pressure to the edges until everything snaps into place. There is a clip near the center of the keyboard so be sure to engage that one as well.
  28. Flip the device over and re-apply the 11 screws that hold the keyboard to the base. Re-apply the rubber feet that cover them.
  29. Update the ticket and send a bill, if applicable.
