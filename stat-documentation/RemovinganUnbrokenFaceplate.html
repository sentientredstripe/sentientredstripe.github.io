Removing an Unbroken Faceplate

Sometimes, by some miracle of skill and/or luck, someone will manage to break their LCD without cracking or damaging the glass in any way. It is an impressive feat and does not happen often. Only attempt this repair if you are experienced in repairing broken A35 faceplates.

Before repairing any device, verify that the student whose Chromebook you are about to repair does not have insurance.

Materials:

  • Pry tool or small flathead screwdriver
  • New LCD
  • New faceplate (in case things do not work out)
  • Space heater, hairdryer, or heatgun
    • The only thing on that list the STAT office is a space heater.
  • Patience


Time: 5-10 minutes


  1. This is a complex repair. It may result in failure (ie a broken faceplate).
  2. Set the space heater to its highest level. The office space heater goes to 90 degrees Fahrenheit.
  3. There is glue holding the faceplate to the display on all four sides. Hold the device so the top of the faceplate is being heated. Hold it there until the top of the glass is hot to the touch.
  4. Immediately set the computer down on a desk and insert your pry tool between the glass of the faceplate and the plastic of the backplate. Gently pry outwards. If the glass does not come off the backplate easily, heat it up and try again.
  5. Do that same procedure for the sides. Be sure not to let the glass re-adhere to the backplate.
  6. For the bottom, simply heat up the glass until it is hot to the touch and gently pull the entire faceplate away from the backplate of the display assembly.
  7. Replace the LCD and re-attach the faceplate.
  8. Update the ticket and send a bill, if applicable.
