LCD Repair

LCD repairs are a constant source of work for STAT.  Therefore, you will get quite good at them very quickly.  Luckily, Dell Chromebook 11 3120 LCDs are very easy to replace.  Before repairing any device, verify that the student whose Chromebook you are about to repair does not have insurance.


Materials:

  • Screwdriver
  • Pry tool
  • New LCD
  • Parts tray

Time: 1-5 minutes


  1. Turn off the device, if you have already not done so.
  2. Open the lid of the Chromebook so the display lays flat on the table.  This will help with Steps 3 and 4.
  3. Locate the plastic stickers on the upper left and right hand corners of the display assembly.  
  4. Using a small screwdriver or pry tool, remove the stickers you just located and store them on your parts tray.
  5. Remove the two screws revealed by removing the stickers and store them on your parts tray.
  6. Using a flathead screwdriver, pry between the black rubber edge and greyish plastic bezel of the display.  The plastic clips should start to disengage.
    1. If you are a beginner, worried about breaking something, or just want to be careful, continue using your screwdriver to pry up the bezel, working your way around both sides and the top.   
    2. If you are more confident in your abilities or just like to YOLO things, pry up enough of the bezel that you can get a good grip with your fingers.  Then, grab the bezel and pull up towards you while simultaneously pulling towards the keyboard.
    3. By this point you should have removed the side and top bezels.  The bottom bezel is slightly more complicated.  To remove it, slide your fingers under the bottom bezel and pull up (or out, depending on your orientation).  If you break a few of the plastic clips that hold it in place, well, that's life.
  7. Now that the bezel is off, locate the four screws holding the display in place.  Remove these and keep them organized.  Be sure not accidentally unscrew the screws holding the frame of the display in place.
  8. The display will fold over onto the keyboard to reveal the back of the display and the display connector.
  9. Remove the piece of tape holding the display connector in place and disconnect the display.
    2. Dispose of the broken display.
  10. Place your new display in the same position the old display was in when you disconnected the cable.  Plug in the new display and fold it up into the display assembly, making sure that the display cable is tucked into its channel.
  11. Reattach the four screws that hold the LCD in place and power on the device to make sure your screen works and you plugged in everything right. Also remove the protective covering from the LCD.
  12. Attach the bezel, making sure to snap all of the clips back into place.  It may take some force, but don't worry about hurting anything.
  13. Reattach the two screws that hold the bezel in place and replace the stickers that cover them.  If you damaged them a lot while taking them off or they simply won't stick, don't worry about it.  They serve no purpose other than to make the bezel look good.
  14. Update the ticket and send a bill.
